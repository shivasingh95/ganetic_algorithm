Step 1 of 7 — The Problem
What is the Travelling
Salesman Problem?
A salesman must visit N cities exactly once and return home. The goal is to find the shortest possible route.
WHY IS IT HARD?
For N cities, there are (N−1)! / 2 possible routes.
10 cities → 181,440 routes
20 cities → 60 trillion routes
30 cities → more than atoms in the universe
Routes = (N−1)! ÷ 2
Step 2 of 7 — The Solution
What is a Genetic Algorithm?
Inspired by Darwin's Theory of Evolution — nature's way of finding optimal solutions over generations.
BiologyGenetic Algorithm
IndividualA route (candidate solution)
DNA / ChromosomeOrder of cities visited
FitnessShorter route = more fit
ReproductionCrossover of two routes
MutationRandom city swaps
Natural selectionKeep best routes

Step 4 of 7 — Evaluation
Step 2: Evaluate Fitness
Calculate the total distance of every route. Shorter distance = higher fitness = better survival chance.
Fitness = 1 ÷ Total Distance
🏆 Route A wins — shortest distance, highest fitness
❌ Route F loses — longest path, lowest fitness
Step 5 of 7 — Reproduction
Step 3: Crossover + Mutation
ORDERED CROSSOVER (OX1)
Copy a random slice from Parent 1. Fill remaining cities from Parent 2 in order — no city visited twice!
SWAP MUTATION
With small probability, randomly swap two cities. Adds diversity, prevents getting stuck.
Step 6 of 7 — The Full Loop
The Evolution Cycle
Each generation, the population gets smarter. Repeat for hundreds of generations until the route can't improve further.

Step 7 of 7 — Why This Works
Why Genetic Algorithms Win
🔍
Brute Force
Checks every route. Exact but impossibly slow for N>20.
O(N!)
🧬
Genetic Algorithm
Evolves good solutions. Fast & near-optimal even for N=100+.
O(G × P × N)
📈
Greedy Heuristic
Always picks nearest city. Fast but gets stuck locally.
O(N²)
